Engagement Format

Deep Dive

90 minutes. Extended exploration of a single theme with interactive discussion and real-time analysis. More rigorous than a keynote, more focused than a workshop. Ideal for leadership teams that need to go beyond the surface.

Deep Dive Topics

AI Economics: Where Value Actually Accrues

A detailed examination of AI's economic structure — the cost curves, the competitive dynamics, who captures value and who gets commoditised. Built for audiences who need to make capital allocation decisions, not just follow the narrative.

Your Sector's AI Inflection Point

Every industry has a specific AI timeline and a specific set of competitive risks. This session maps yours — using Azeem's framework applied to your sector's supply chain, talent structure, and customer economics.

Governing AI: What Boards Need to Know

A structured session on AI oversight for boards and audit committees. Covers AI risk taxonomy, governance models adopted by leading organisations, and the board-level questions that matter most in 2025 and beyond.

The Long View: AI in 5 and 10 Years

A longer-horizon session examining where AI is likely to go — based on the underlying technology trends, economic incentives, and historical patterns from previous general-purpose technologies. For leadership teams setting 5-year strategy.

Session Structure

Argument, evidence, discussion

A deep dive is structured around a single argument, developed fully. Azeem takes a position — based on evidence from his research, investing, and advisory work — and tests it with the room.

The format rewards intellectually curious audiences who want to interrogate the argument, not just receive it.

0–20 min

The argument

Azeem states his thesis clearly and lays out the evidence base. No hedging.

20–50 min

Second and third-order effects for your industry, your organisation, your strategy.

Unpacking the implications

50–75 min

Structured Q&A

Prepared questions from your team interleaved with open discussion. Azeem responds to challenges, updates his view where warranted.

75–90 min

So what?

Explicit synthesis: given all of this, what are the 3–4 things your organisation should actually do differently?
