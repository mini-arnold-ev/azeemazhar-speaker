For Financial Services

The investment lens on AI — from someone who operates with it, invests in it, and tracks the underlying economics.

Azeem Azhar works with investment banks, sovereign wealth funds, private equity firms, and asset managers on the questions that matter most: where returns will concentrate in the AI economy, how to read the compute and energy constraints, and how to act decisively under genuine uncertainty.

Founder of Exponential View & technology investor · Author, The Exponential Age (FT Best Book 2021) · Executive Fellow, Harvard Business School · Digital Fellow, Stanford University · Co-Chair, WEF Global Future Council on Next Generation Computing

Track Record

For a decade, Azeem has applied Wright's Law cost curves to identify technology inflections before consensus — from naming GPT-3 as a knowledge breakthrough in 2020 to flagging energy as AI's binding constraint in the New York Times in 2024, typically 12–24 months ahead of the market.

His work spans the full technology-capital interface: as founder of Exponential View (read by 286,000 decision-makers including economists, fund managers, and technology executives), as an active investor in the AI infrastructure layer, and as a practitioner who operates a multi-agent AI system as the backbone of his own work.

He is not a technologist who learned finance, or a financier who learned AI. He works at the intersection by design — and his frameworks reflect that.

Session Topics

Built around investment decisions, not technology narratives

The AI Supercycle: Where We Are and What Comes Next

A structured view of the current AI investment cycle — distinguishing hype from structural shift, mapping the infrastructure buildout, and identifying where returns will concentrate and where they will be competed away. Built on Wright's Law cost curves and historical technology diffusion data.

Compute, Energy, and the Physics of AI Investment

AI scaling is now constrained by energy and semiconductor supply, not model architecture. This session covers the compute bottleneck thesis, energy infrastructure as the next capital allocation decision, and the geopolitics of the semiconductor supply chain — for portfolio companies and direct infrastructure investment alike.

The Geopolitics of the Technology Stack

US-China bifurcation of AI infrastructure, sovereign AI stack strategies, and the investment implications of a world where technology systems are increasingly nation-state assets. Particularly relevant for global allocation decisions and sovereign investors.

Agentic AI: The Capital Allocation Question

When AI agents begin acting autonomously at scale — executing trades, running compliance workflows, sourcing deals — what does that mean for financial services business models, staffing economics, and competitive position? A framework for the decisions that need to be made in the next 18 months.

Navigating Uncertainty Without False Clarity

A session built specifically for senior leaders making decisions under genuine uncertainty. Drawing on the concept of 'equipped ambiguity,' this format helps leadership teams act decisively on AI strategy without pretending to resolve what cannot yet be resolved. Designed for partner offsites, LP days, and board sessions.

Every session is built around your institution's specific investment thesis and portfolio context. Generic talks are available elsewhere.

Formats

Structured for how financial services organisations actually make decisions

Partner & LP Offsite

1–4 hour format for 10–50 senior professionals. A structured provocation followed by facilitated dialogue. The thinking-environment format that generates the sharpest strategic questions.

Suitable for: GP teams, investment committees, LP relationship events

Leadership Keynote

20–45 minutes for large client or internal audiences. A complete analytical argument with implications. Built around your audience's sector and investment thesis, not generic.

Suitable for: Client conferences, annual leadership forums, global all-hands

Intimate Dinner / Roundtable

8–20 people. The highest-signal format. Structured around 2–3 contested questions. Participants leave with frameworks, not just perspectives.

Suitable for: Senior client dinners, advisory board sessions, CIO forums

Research Briefing

90 minutes with an investment team. Deep-dive on a specific thesis — AI infrastructure, energy transition, agentic economy — with Q&A structured around portfolio implications.

Suitable for: Research teams, thematic investment units, new fund formation
