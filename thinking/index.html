Knowledge Shifting from Fragile Stock to Compounding Flow

Knowledge is shifting from extraction (trapped in biological containers, expensive to produce and transfer, dies with people) to manufacturing (produced at collapsing cost, distributed instantly, recursively compounding). Learning curves are the mechanism: as knowledge compounds, the cost of applying it falls and the speed of generating new knowledge increases. Previously closed doors become visible. Knowledge, now radically cheaper, dissolves constraints into doors. This is the core claim of Infinite Growth.

HIGH CONFIDENCE STRUCTURAL Reviewed 16 days ago

Economic Growth Shifting from Extraction-Limited to Knowledge-Limited

The binding constraint on economic growth is moving from extraction (more output requires proportionally more inputs) to knowledge (value creation decouples from resource consumption). Whether growth behaves like cancer or compounding knowledge depends on where the bottleneck sits. Chips need silicon, but silicon is abundant — the constraint is lithography breakthroughs. This pattern is generalising across sectors. Allocation frameworks designed for extraction-limited growth systematically misread knowledge-limited growth.

Energy surplus is the prerequisite for everything beyond survival. The shift from extraction (fossil fuels, geography-dependent) to manufactured generation (solar, wind) is creating the largest energy surplus in human history. This surplus is not incremental — it changes what is possible. Societies that capture it first will compound advantages across every other domain.
